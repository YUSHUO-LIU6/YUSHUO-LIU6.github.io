## About me

I am a master's student in Sport Pedagogy and Training at the School of Physical Education, Yunnan Normal University, China. My current research focuses on the intersection of **sprint and jump biomechanics, lower-limb tendon loading, exercise-based rehabilitation, and training intervention studies**.

My academic background is in Exercise and Human Science. During my undergraduate study at Shenyang Sport University, I gained experience in rehabilitation practice and research assistance, including stroke rehabilitation, lower-limb rehabilitation training, motion analysis-related experiments, and movement model construction.

I am currently involved in the scientific support, strength and conditioning, and rehabilitation work of the university track and field team. I use **Kinovea** to analyze technical variables in long jump and triple jump athletes, including penultimate-step characteristics, knee joint angles, center-of-mass-related variables, and take-off-related parameters.

My long-term goal is to pursue a funded PhD position in Europe in **applied sport biomechanics, sprint and jump performance, tendon rehabilitation, or exercise intervention research**.

## Current research projects

I am currently working on the following projects:

**1. Horizontal versus vertical plyometric training for sprint performance**

I am conducting a randomized controlled trial investigating the effects of horizontal and vertical plyometric training on sprint performance and lower-limb functional outcomes in male university 200 m and 400 m athletes. The study protocol has been completed, baseline testing has been conducted, and the intervention is ongoing.

**2. Patellar tendon responses to training load in jumping athletes**

I am preparing a review on patellar tendon responses to training load in jumping athletes. This project is registered on OSF and aims to connect tendon adaptation, jumping sports, training load monitoring, injury prevention, and exercise-based rehabilitation.

**3. Individualized lower-limb ischemic preconditioning and sprint performance**

I am developing a randomized, sham-controlled, crossover study examining the acute effects of individualized lower-limb ischemic preconditioning combined with horizontal plyometric activation on short-distance sprint performance in male sport science students. The protocol has been completed and registered on OSF.

**4. Plyometric training transfer to athletic performance**

I am planning a three-level meta-analysis and meta-regression on the specificity of transfer from plyometric training to athletic performance, with a focus on force vector, support mode, and stretch-shortening cycle demands.

**5. Field-based motion analysis for sprint and jump biomechanics**

I am interested in field-based and low-cost motion analysis methods, including Kinovea, OpenCap, OpenPose, MediaPipe, and other markerless motion capture tools. I aim to examine how these methods can be used in sprint and jump biomechanics, especially for practical applications in training and rehabilitation settings.
